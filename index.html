AI Agents: Explained Simply

The Key Analogy

📚

Chatbot = Librarian

You ask, it retrieves information

🧮

AI Agent = Accountant

Reasons through problems, makes plans, takes action

The Agent Spectrum

Rule-Based Bot

If X → do Y

Chatbot

Understands language, retrieves answers

AI Agent

Reasons, plans, uses tools

Autonomous Agent

Self-directed, multi-step goals

What Makes It an "Agent"?

AI Agent = LLM + Tools + Guidance

LLM (Brain)

Understands language, reasons about problems

Tools (Hands)

Can search databases, send emails, update systems

Guidance (Instructions)

Rules about when to act, when to escalate to humans

Common Misconceptions (Address These Early)

✗

"AI always improves productivity"

Reality: Gains require thoughtful implementation. Not every process benefits equally.

✗

"Full automation without oversight"

Reality: Agents need training, fine-tuning, and human oversight — especially for sensitive decisions.

✗

"AI will replace entire workforces"

Reality: Only ~5% of occupations could be fully automated. AI reshapes roles, not erases them.

✗

"Only large enterprises can benefit"

Reality: No-code tools like Copilot Studio make agents accessible to any team size.

✗

"One-size-fits-all solutions work"

Reality: Customization is mandatory for real value. Generic agents deliver generic results.

Cross-Functional Use Cases: Operations, Finance & Legal

OP

Operations

  • Supply Chain Tracker: Agent monitors vendor SLAs, flags delays, auto-escalates missed deadlines to procurement leads.
  • Incident Triage: Classifies incoming service tickets by severity, routes to correct team, and suggests resolution steps from knowledge base.
  • Workforce Scheduling: Answers shift-swap requests, checks coverage rules, and surfaces gaps before they become outages.
  • SOP Assistant: Employees ask "How do I process a tower lease renewal?" and get step-by-step guidance from internal SOPs.
FN

Finance

  • Invoice Query Bot: Vendors and internal teams ask "What's the status of PO #4521?" — agent pulls data from ERP and responds instantly.
  • Expense Policy Agent: Employees check "Can I expense a client dinner over $200?" — agent validates against policy and provides approval workflow.
  • Revenue Leakage Alerts: Monitors billing anomalies — unbilled usage, plan mismatches, expired promotions still applied — and flags for review.
  • Budget Q&A: Department heads ask "How much of Q3 marketing budget is remaining?" — agent pulls real-time data from finance systems.
LG

Legal

  • Contract Review Assistant: Scans vendor contracts for non-standard clauses, liability caps, and auto-renewal traps — surfaces red flags before signing.
  • Regulatory FAQ Agent: Staff ask "Do we need GDPR consent for this campaign in Bulgaria?" — agent references jurisdiction-specific compliance docs.
  • NDA & Template Agent: Sales teams request "I need an NDA for a partner in Serbia" — agent selects correct template, pre-fills known fields, routes for approval.
  • Policy Compliance Checker: Employees describe a planned action, agent checks against internal policies and flags potential violations before they happen.

MS Copilot Studio: Hands-On Exercises

Five ready-to-use exercises for the 30-minute hands-on segment. Each is designed for complete beginners with no coding required.

What is Copilot Studio?

A no-code/low-code SaaS platform where you describe your agent in plain English, and the AI generates your agent's name, instructions, knowledge sources, and conversation flows. No programming required. 1,400+ pre-built connectors to Teams, Outlook, Dynamics, ServiceNow, etc.

A

Telecom Customer FAQ Bot

35 minutes | Recommended for session
1

Go to copilotstudio.microsoft.com and sign in with your work/school account

2

Click "Create" → select "New agent"

3

In the description box, type: "Help customers of Yettel find answers about billing, plan changes, network coverage, and SIM replacement"

4

Accept the AI-generated name/instructions or tweak them to your liking

5

Add Knowledge: Click "Knowledge" → "Add" → choose "Files" — upload the prepared FAQ document (10-15 common telecom Q&As)

6

Create 3 Topics: Billing Inquiry, Plan Change, Network Issue — each with trigger phrases and simple message responses

7

Test: Use the built-in test panel to ask questions like "My bill seems too high" or "How do I change my plan?"

8

Publish: Deploy to a demo website with one click

Facilitation Tips

Show the "before"

Start by showing the manual process first, then demonstrate the AI alternative. Makes the value immediately tangible.

Same tool for everyone

Have all participants use the same AI tool to reduce troubleshooting friction and enable peer support.

End with "Show & Tell"

Have 2-3 participants demo their agents. Builds pride, sparks ideas, and creates social proof within the group.
